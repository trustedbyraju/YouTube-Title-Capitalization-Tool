What is YouTube Optimized Title Case?

YouTube Optimized Title Case follows standard English title capitalization guidelines. Major words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs) are capitalized, while short "function" words — articles (a, an, the), short prepositions (in, on, at, by, for, of, to, up, with), and conjunctions (and, but, or, nor, so, yet) — are kept lowercase unless they are the first or last word of the title.

What words are kept lowercase in Title Case?

In our YouTube Optimized Title Case, the following short words are kept lowercase (unless they start the title): Articles: a, an, the. Prepositions: at, by, for, from, in, of, on, to, up, with, into, onto, upon, via. Coordinating conjunctions: and, but, or, nor, so, yet, as.

The 5 Capitalization Styles for YouTube Titles

Understanding the different styles helps you choose the right one for your brand and content type:

  1. Title Case (YouTube Optimized) — The gold standard for YouTube. Major words are capitalized; short articles, prepositions, and conjunctions are lowercase (unless at the start). Example: "How to Make Money on YouTube in 2026"
  2. Sentence case — Only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized. This style is common on platforms like Medium and feels conversational. Example: "How to make money on YouTube in 2026"
  3. UPPERCASE (ALL CAPS) — Every letter capitalized. Use sparingly — it can feel like shouting and may be perceived as clickbait by some audiences. Example: "HOW TO MAKE MONEY ON YOUTUBE IN 2026"
  4. lowercase — No capitalization at all. Can work for ultra-casual, Gen Z-oriented channels, but generally reduces professionalism. Example: "how to make money on youtube in 2026"
  5. Capitalize Each Word — Every single word capitalized regardless of its grammatical role. Slightly less nuanced than Title Case but still looks clean. Example: "How To Make Money On YouTube In 2026"

YouTube Title Capitalization Rules: What to Capitalize

When using Title Case (the recommended style), follow these rules for words to capitalize:

  • The first and last word of every title, always
  • Nouns: YouTube, camera, money, tutorial, guide
  • Verbs: Make, Build, Create, Learn, Start
  • Adjectives: Best, Free, Ultimate, Complete
  • Adverbs: Quickly, Easily, Instantly, Finally
  • Numbers and numerals: 10, 2026, #1

And words to keep lowercase (unless at the start):

  • Articles: a, an, the
  • Short prepositions: at, by, for, from, in, of, on, to, up, with, into, onto, via
  • Coordinating conjunctions: and, but, or, nor, so, yet, as

How Title Capitalization Affects YouTube SEO

YouTube's search algorithm analyzes title text for keyword relevance. While capitalization itself doesn't directly affect keyword matching (YouTube is case-insensitive for search), it profoundly affects human behavior — specifically whether a person chooses to click your video over a competitor's.

Research in user experience and cognitive psychology consistently shows that text formatted with consistent, expected capitalization patterns is easier and faster to read. Easier-to-read titles mean viewers make click decisions faster, which correlates with higher CTR. In competitive niches where multiple videos target the same keyword, this can be the deciding factor in which video YouTube promotes.

Common YouTube Title Capitalization Mistakes to Avoid

  • Random capitalization: "how To make MONEY On YouTube" — looks like a typo or spam
  • All lowercase on professional channels: Fine for casual creators, but can undermine authority in educational or business niches
  • Overusing ALL CAPS: Studies show viewers associate excessive all-caps with low-quality clickbait
  • Inconsistency across videos: Mix of styles across your channel creates an unprofessional impression
  • Capitalizing articles/prepositions needlessly: "How To Make Money On YouTube" vs. the more refined "How to Make Money on YouTube"

The Optimal YouTube Title Formula for 2026

Beyond capitalization, a high-performing YouTube title in 2026 typically combines several elements: a primary keyword near the front, a clear value proposition or emotional hook, and the right length (60–70 characters). Here's a formula that works:

[Number or Power Word] + [Primary Keyword] + [Value/Benefit] + [Year or Qualifier]

Example: "10 Proven Ways to Grow Your YouTube Channel in 2026" — keyword-rich, value-clear, properly formatted.
